Soft Margin Support Vector Machines

If our data is not completely linearly separable due to a few points, depsite still looking like a linear classifer would generally do a good job, we can use Soft Margin SVMs.
These are a type of Support Vector Machine that allows for a certain amount of error during training. The amount of error is known as C or the Hinge Loss. The use of this parameter is a tradeoff between having a large margin and correctly classifying the training data. You get better classification of training data at the expense of a wide margin. By choosing a higher value of C you imply that you want less errors on the training data.
